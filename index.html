Component Obsolescence

A sourcing challenge

A presentation by Zsolt Kazsi

Definitions / Differences

Excess & obsolete components

VS.

Component obsolescence - long term availability

Products are made obsolete by their users...

...resulting in obsolescence of the components

Definition

"The loss or impending loss of manufacturers of items or suppliers of items or raw materials."

Industry effects

  • Contradiction: availability of components vs. expected lifecycle
  • Final product expected to live 10 - 20 - 30 years
  • Implicit responsibility of OEM, yet no ongoing involvement

Obsolete components: 80% semiconductors

  • Moore's Law
  • Average lifecycle is 4 years
  • Market driven by other sectors

How to handle

Reactive (fire fighting) or preventive (fire prevention)

Sourcing: case study

EMS Partner: strongest worldwide

  • ca 30% of annual spend
  • ad hoc requests traditionally
  • high mix low volume

Long term reliable solution required

Results

Sales: basis for success: 4 projects today (CH, UK, FI)

Supplier: less discussion about scope
